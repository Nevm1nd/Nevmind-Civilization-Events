Nevmind’s 100 Player Civilization Event:
A Tale of Power, Betrayal, and Survival

In an ambitious Minecraft experiment hosted by Nevmind, 100 players were thrust into a custom-crafted world divided across three massive and distinct islands: the lush and resource-rich Plains, the desolate and scorching Desert, and the cold, mountainous Snow Island. Each player was given one life—death meant permanent elimination. This high-stakes structure gave rise to one of the most compelling player-driven events in Minecraft history, unfolding over several dramatic in-game days.

Day One: Foundations of Civilization

The event opened with players scattered across the islands, each forced to adapt to their respective environments. The Plains offered abundant food, wood, and animals, attracting early settlements like Oaklia, a city that rapidly grew under the leadership of DiamondDawgMC.

In the Snow biome, two rival yet peaceful nations formed: the Tundra Organizations (TTO), led by Shurwin, and the Vodka Nations (TVN), led by the charismatic MRNT. Despite limited resources, these nations began building infrastructure and even considered diplomatic engagement.

Meanwhile, the Desert’s harsh climate demanded unity. Through the efforts of 127K and ROCOplays, nearly all Desert players united under one flag: the LLW (Large Land Walkers). But LLW’s political future quickly took center stage when an election was called to determine its leader. Against all odds, the relatively unknown Mhitthraw defeated 127K to become the head of the largest nation on the server.

However, the election was marred by the disruptive antics of a new and unpredictable figure: Peter_Barker, who loudly and repeatedly interrupted the proceedings in a failed attempt to seize power. His presence would prove to be a harbinger of chaos.

Day Two: The Rise of Conflict

As the second day began, the Plains and Snow Islands enjoyed relative peace and progress. On the Plains, Oaklia expanded. The TVN managed to cure and house its first villager—an economic turning point.

The Desert, however, descended into turmoil.

Peter_Barker formed a small but deadly faction known as the E.A.G. (Elite Assassination Group), recruiting highly skilled PvP players and establishing a base near LLW territory. Tensions exploded after Barker attacked LLW members and later clashed with them when they pursued an intruder into his territory. In a shocking display of force, the E.A.G. defeated LLW fighters and began earning a fearsome reputation.

Discontent grew inside LLW, especially after multiple failed encounters with the E.A.G. Confidence in Mhitthraw’s leadership faltered, and LLW began hemorrhaging members. Among the disillusioned was 127K, who founded a breakaway faction: The Northern Civilization. Their plan? Defect to Oaklia when the walls between islands fell the following day.

Meanwhile, Mhitthraw plotted revenge. In a risky move, he approached both Barker and MRNT, proposing a secret plan: lure the E.A.G. into attacking TVN, then double-cross them with a coordinated ambush. MRNT agreed—but the plan unraveled.

Day Three: Betrayal and Bloodshed

As the barriers between islands fell, 127K and his followers successfully defected to Oaklia and were granted land. At the same time, Mhitthraw set his trap in motion. Barker agreed to raid TVN with LLW support. But when a warning about an E.A.G. spy reached MRNT, the TVN leadership evacuated, abandoning the trap.

Frustrated, Mhitthraw launched a solo attack against the E.A.G.—and was killed.

The betrayal failed, and Peter_Barker emerged stronger than ever.

With renewed momentum, the E.A.G. turned its attention to Oaklia. Though Oaklia was the most developed nation in the game and backed by allies from TVN and TTO, the E.A.G. launched a devastating guerrilla-style campaign. In quick succession, they assassinated several Oaklian soldiers and even killed Oaklia’s leader, DiamondDawgMC, during a dramatic boat chase.

Barker’s most shocking feat came during a 1v6 skirmish, where he single-handedly defeated six Oaklian soldiers—including 127K, whose death marked the symbolic collapse of the Northern Civilization’s hopes.

The Plains alliance—Oaklia, TVN, and TTO—was shattered. Their military strength was gutted, their morale devastated. The E.A.G. had become a near-mythical threat. Despite their small numbers, they had overcome every challenge, outwitted every opponent, and shifted the balance of power across the server.

Conclusion: The Hunt Begins

As Day Three drew to a close, the remnants of Oaklia, TVN, and TTO regrouped. In the wake of their losses, the players rallied around a final purpose: to bring Peter_Barker to justice.

“This marks the continuation of our mission. Justice must be served. Vengeance for the fallen must be claimed. And peace must be restored.”
— Arqs, second-in-command of TTO

The server now teetered on the edge of open war. What began as a simple civilization event had become something far greater—an unfolding saga of strategy, betrayal, politics, and war.

One hundred players entered a world where they were free to shape their own destinies. Few could have predicted how far that freedom would take them.

And as for Peter_Barker?

The hunt continues.
