The Demise of the Desert Giant:
How the LLW Collapsed in Nevmind's Civilization Event

In the sprawling, imaginative world of Nevmind's 100 Player Civilization Event, where participants were granted complete freedom and only one life, The LLW (The Lame Loser Wankers) emerged as a formidable force on the Desert Island. However, its meteoric rise was soon followed by a dramatic and complete collapse, leaving it in ruins by Day Three of the event.

Day One: The Birth of a Desert Powerhouse

On Day One, the Desert Island was characterized by endless sand and cacti, with vital patches of fertile soil and trees only found along certain parts of the shore. To ensure safety and survival in this harsh environment, two influential players, ROCOplays and 127K, spearheaded recruitment efforts, advocating for the unity of all Desert players under a single large nation and leadership. Their efforts were remarkably successful. By the end of the very first day, The LLW was established, uniting roughly 30 players—almost every single player on the Desert Island—under one banner, making it the largest nation on the entire server.

Initially, the LLW focused on gathering resources, crafting gear, and establishing infrastructure. With the sheer number of members, the group needed strong leadership. Rather than appointing themselves rulers, ROCOplays and 127K called for the first democratic election of the event.

Two candidates ran for leadership:

  • 127K: Co-founder and experienced strategist. He promoted military strength for long-term stability.
  • Mhitthraw91: A regular citizen who promised internal reforms, food supply improvements, and fair leadership.

ROCOplays supported 127K but did not run. The election was briefly disrupted by a third party, Peter_Barker, who attempted to claim power with no legitimacy. Despite the disturbance, Mhitthraw won by a wide margin and became the official leader of the LLW.

Day Two: The E.A.G. Emerges and Escalates Tensions

At the beginning of Day Two, Peter_Barker gathered a group of elite PvP players near LLW’s borders, forming the Elite Assassination Group (E.A.G.). Tensions flared when Barker allegedly attacked two LLW members and later defended a rogue player, Delayedshade, who stole from the LLW settlement and fled toward E.A.G. territory.

LLW forces retaliated, entering E.A.G. land and launching an attack. However, E.A.G.’s skillful defense led to a humiliating LLW defeat, with several members killed. The loss severely damaged LLW’s reputation and morale, especially given their numerical superiority.

Discontent within LLW grew. A follow-up clash near the tree farms resulted in further deaths, widening internal fractures. Mhitthraw attempted diplomacy, but trust in his leadership faltered. As defections increased, 127K seized the moment, forming the Northern Civilization—a splinter group that planned to flee the Desert and ally with the Plains nation Oaklia when island barriers dropped on Day Three.

Meanwhile, Mhitthraw concocted a final plan to salvage the LLW. He approached both E.A.G. and TVN’s leader, MRNT, proposing a double-cross ambush against the E.A.G. during a raid on TVN. The deal was accepted, and the stage was set.

Day Three: Betrayal and Bloodshed

As Day Three began and the island walls fell, the Northern Civilization migrated to Oaklia, abandoning the LLW. Simultaneously, the E.A.G. began raiding TVN territory, unaware of Mhitthraw’s betrayal plan.

However, fearing a potential E.A.G. spy, MRNT evacuated TVN’s players before the ambush could take place. Left without backup, Mhitthraw launched a solo attack on the E.A.G.—and was swiftly killed.

The failed ambush exposed the conspiracy, and Peter_Barker’s E.A.G. emerged stronger than ever.

Conclusion: From Powerhouse to Ruins

Mhitthraw’s death marked the final collapse of the LLW. The once-mighty nation, founded with unity and purpose, had crumbled under the weight of internal conflict, military defeat, and poor leadership. The group disbanded, its members scattered. The LLW, which had once been the most populous and promising civilization, now existed only as a memory—a failed empire buried beneath the sands of the Desert Island.

The rise and fall of the LLW remains one of the most dramatic stories in Nevmind’s Civilization Event history.
